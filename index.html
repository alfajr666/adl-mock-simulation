[01] WHAT IS ADL

Tear-Ups (TradFi Origin)

In traditional futures markets, clearing houses use "tear-ups" to forcibly close offsetting positions when a participant can no longer meet obligations. ADL in crypto is the direct structural equivalent — not a punitive measure, but a necessity to prevent continued loss accumulation from an insolvent counterparty.

Variation Margin Gains Haircutting

When a loser can't pay, someone must cover the gap. VMGH takes money from winners to cover bad debt. The alternative—requiring FCM brokers to post their own capital—kills leverage products entirely by disincentivizing brokers from offering them.

Insurance Fund: First Line

Exchanges build an insurance fund from liquidation fees. When a position is closed worse than bankruptcy price, the fund absorbs the gap. The vast majority of liquidations never reach ADL. Only when the fund is depleted does the exchange reach into the ALP queue.

ADL: Structural Last Resort

ADL fires only when a position is liquidated AND the insurance fund cannot cover the resulting loss gap. Profitable positions on the opposite side are closed at the bankrupt trader's last positive-equity price. Well-designed ADL aids market stability.

WHY TWO SETTLEMENT PRICES?

  Liquidated position (loser)  →  closed at bankruptPrice
    = last mark price where equity was ≥ 0
    = restores account to zero, no more

  ALP position (winner)  →  closed at currentMarkPrice
    = converts unrealized PnL to realized at fair value
    = no haircut beyond what closing the gap requires
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────
  Asymmetric (loser at bankruptPrice, winner at currentMarkPrice):
    The only mathematically correct approach.
    Exchange absorbs only the true gap: 
    |bankruptPrice − currentMarkPrice| × qty.
LEGACY HEURISTIC (Binance, Bybit, OKX)
    ADL Priority = PnL% × Effective Leverage
    
    Flaw: profit magnitude distorts ranking independent 
    of systemic risk. Disproportionately penalizes new traders.
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────
  QFEX METHOD (ak0, QFEX design)
    ADL Priority = Effective Leverage only
    
    Logic: ADL's objective is systemic de-risking. 
    Highest effective leverage = highest contribution to 
    systemic risk = reduced first. Profit is irrelevant.

[06] KEY CONCEPTS

01

ADL Indicator

The 5-pip queue indicator represents your priority bracket on the exchange. 5 pips = you are first to be closed.

02

Lower Leverage = Lower Risk

Under correctly designed ADL engines (QFEX), maintaining low effective leverage guarantees safety.

03

Take Partial Profits

Because unrealized profits act as equity, realizing gains resets your effective leverage and drops you down the queue.

High Risk Periods
